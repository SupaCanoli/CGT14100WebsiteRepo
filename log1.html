I've finally been approved to begin work on my new project, therefore I begin my writing a personal log system for the first time in a long time. It's been so long since anyone at Strongarm Corps has been able to make their own project, instead of just having us design more cybernetics like always. It's nearly impossible to find a company in this city that will let you have ownership of your own innovation anymore. Not like I can do anything about it, considering I need the money. Regardless, this isn't another cybernetic upgrade project, which is a nice breath of fresh air for me. I've also made a good friend that I can at least talk to who won't sell out my information before I even figure out if what I've been working on is even worth anything.

Angela is a very capable coworker of mine, especially for working in this corporation for only 5 months. She has been a great way to get some of my ideas out of my head to someone else for once without them immediately being taken for the benefit of a company that has no care for them as I do. She's been a wonderful help to my work, and I can't be more grateful to get to know her as I have thus far.

Regardless of this, today I need to finally put all of my research towards my new idea into action. I've been working towards a new concept that I can't seem to scour any information about online or in any sort of areas nearby either. I found basic concepts here and there, but it seems to be very underdeveloped. My concept in question for this project is nanotechnology. This is something that I think may help the type of people we're trying to assist, especially in fixing issues that are small enough to not be easily fixed by an amputated and replaced limb for example.

My idea might seem a bit far-fetched to some of the people I work with if they knew, but I firmly believe that it might be more feasible than we think to make a type of... humanoid, for lack of better wording? I want to make a type of human being that is fused with nanotechnology, in order to have a set of robots that can fix problems in the human body on the fly. The main problem my information I've gathered so far seems to be is that the human body will inherently reject anything it doesn't recognize as it's own. My running theory is that making something that can personally fuse this nanotechnology into the cells of a human being might fix needing to have a whole separate system being added to the human body. The working name I've given this theoretical body is "B1/0". It's a bit ambiguous, all things considered, but it helps me remember some of the things in my notes better without needing to refer to them every single time I want to test something about them.

My new problem after doing all my research and simulative testing is the need for a physical test in order to prove that funding my idea might actually work. I've been approved to actually put my work forward into physical testing, but the company has not taken light to my idea very much with how little info I've given them to work with. I can't prove viability to them unless I tell them more detail on the project, and they certainly aren't the kind of people to that can be argued to while still staying on good terms. I just have to hope I find some way to prove it's viability without compromising it from being my work of passion... I'll come back to this when I have an update.

~ Yours patiently, Supa.



Update:

It's been nearly 2 whole months since I've had all my research proved to myself and prepared. The company still will not let me have any sort of subject to test on without explaining in more detail to them how my innovation works. I have been trying my absolute hardest not to let them know everything, even essentially locking myself in my personal lab room that I've been doing all of this work in. A passion project like this feels so wrong to give up this easily. A new idea that's been slowly becoming more enticing in my head has been to just attempt this experiment on myself. All of my research is correct, it just has to. I've checked it over nearly a million times by now. This is feasible. I just can't prove it without letting go of my work.

Screw it. I'm gonna try the experiment on myself. I'll update here once I'm done using the machine I've built to make this as smooth as possible.

~ Yours impatiently, Supa.
